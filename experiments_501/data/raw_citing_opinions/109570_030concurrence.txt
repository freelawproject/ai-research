<author id="b364-7">Mr. Justice Stewart,</author>
<p id="AW0">concurring in the judgment.</p>
<p id="b364-8">I agree that the appellant Whitener has standing to assert the equal protection claims of males between 18 and 21 years old. <em>Eisenstadt </em>v. <em>Baird, </em><span class="citation" data-id="108489"><a href="/opinion/108489/eisenstadt-v-baird/#443" aria-description="Citation for case: Eisenstadt v. Baird">405 U. S. 438, 443-446</a></span>; <em>Griswold </em>v. <em>Connecticut, </em><span class="citation" data-id="107082"><a href="/opinion/107082/griswold-v-connecticut/#481" aria-description="Citation for case: Griswold v. Connecticut">381 U. S. 479, 481</a></span>; <em>Barrows </em>v. <em>Jackson, </em><span class="citation" data-id="105152"><a href="/opinion/105152/barrows-v-jackson/#255" aria-description="Citation for case: Barrows v. Jackson">346 U. S. 249, 255-260</a></span>; <em>Buchanan </em>v. <em>Warley, </em><span class="citation" data-id="99012"><a href="/opinion/99012/buchanan-v-warley/#72" aria-description="Citation for case: Buchanan v. Warley">245 U. S. 60, 72-73</a></span>; see Note, Standing To Assert Constitutional Jus Tertii, <span class="citation no-link">88 Harv. L. Rev. 423</span>, 431-436 (1974). I also concur in the Court’s judgment on the merits of the constitutional issue before us.</p>
<p id="b365-4"><page-number citation-index="1" label="215">*215</page-number>Every State has broad power under the Twenty-first Amendment to control the dispensation of alcoholic beverages within its borders. <em>E. g., California </em>v. <em>LaRue, </em><span class="citation" data-id="108636"><a href="/opinion/108636/california-v-larue/" aria-description="Citation for case: California v. LaRue">409 U. S. 109</a></span>; <em>Joseph E. Seagram &amp; Sons </em>v. <em>Hostetter, </em><span class="citation" data-id="107217"><a href="/opinion/107217/joseph-e-seagram-sons-inc-v-hostetter/" aria-description="Citation for case: Joseph E. Seagram &amp; Sons, Inc. v. Hostetter">384 U. S. 35</a></span>; <em>Hostetter </em>v. <em>Idlewild Bon Voyage Liquor Corp., </em><span class="citation" data-id="106834"><a href="/opinion/106834/hostetter-v-idlewild-bon-voyage-liquor-corp/#330" aria-description="Citation for case: Hostetter v. Idlewild Bon Voyage Liquor Corp.">377 U. S. 324, 330</a></span>; <em>Mahoney </em>v. <em>Joseph Triner Corp., </em><span class="citation" data-id="103046"><a href="/opinion/103046/mahoney-v-joseph-triner-corp/" aria-description="Citation for case: Mahoney v. Joseph Triner Corp.">304 U. S. 401</a></span>; <em>State Board </em>v. <em>Young’s Market Co., </em><span class="citation" data-id="102694"><a href="/opinion/102694/state-bd-of-equalization-of-cal-v-youngs-market-co/" aria-description="Citation for case: State Bd. of Equalization of Cal. v. Young&#x27;s Market Co.">299 U. S. 59</a></span>. But “[t]his is not to say that the Twenty-first Amendment empowers a State to act with total irrationality or invidious discrimination in controlling the distribution and dispensation of liquor <em>. . . .” California </em>v. <em><span class="citation" data-id="108636"><a href="/opinion/108636/california-v-larue/" aria-description="Citation for case: California v. LaRue">LaRue, supra,</a></span> </em>at 120 n. (concurring opinion).</p>
<p id="b365-5">The disparity created by these Oklahoma statutes amounts to total irrationality. For the statistics upon which the State now relies, whatever their other shortcomings, wholly fail to prove or even suggest that 3.2% beer is somehow more deleterious when it comes into the hands of a male aged 18-20 than of a female of like age. The disparate statutory treatment of the sexes here, without even a colorably valid justification or explanation, thus amounts to invidious discrimination. See <em>Reed </em>v. <em>Reed, </em><span class="citation" data-id="108404"><a href="/opinion/108404/reed-v-reed/" aria-description="Citation for case: Reed v. Reed">404 U. S. 71</a></span>.</p>
