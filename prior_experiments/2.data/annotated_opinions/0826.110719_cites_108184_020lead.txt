After reviewing the relevant authorities at some length, the Court concluded that the probable-cause requirement was satisfied in the case before it. The Court held that “the facts and circumstances within [the officers’] knowledge and of which they had reasonably trustworthy information were sufficient in themselves to warrant a man of reasonable caution in the belief *809 that intoxicating liquor was being transported in the automobile which they stopped and searched.” 267 U. Many of these searches do not require a showing of probable cause that the vehicle contains contraband.
