<p id="b280-9">I join Parts I, II-A, II-C, and III of the Court’s opinion. Because the Court ultimately resolves this case under the <page-number citation-index="1" label="231">*231</page-number>Extradition Act, 18 U. S. C. § 3182, I do not find Part II-B, and its statements concerning the Extradition Clause of the Constitution, necessary to the decision of this case. Accordingly, I do not subscribe to that part of the Court’s opinion. See, <em>e. g., Jean </em>v. <em>Nelson, </em>472 U. S. 846, 854 (1985); <em>Kolender </em>v. <em>Lawson, </em>461 U. S. 352, 361, n. 10 (1983); <em>Ashwander </em>v. <em>TVA, </em>297 U. S. 288, 347 (1936) (Brandeis, J., concurring).</p>
